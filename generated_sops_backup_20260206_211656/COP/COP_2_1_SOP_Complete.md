## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To establish a systematic process for identifying, flagging, and ensuring appropriate care for high-risk patients who require enhanced monitoring, specialized interventions, or additional safety precautions.

**Scope:** This SOP covers identification and care protocols for all categories of high-risk patients from admission through discharge across all clinical units.

**Applicability:**
- All physicians and consultants
- All nursing staff
- Allied health professionals
- Quality and safety officers
- Unit managers

---

## 2. Definitions

- **High-Risk Patient:** A patient who has an increased probability of adverse outcomes and requires enhanced monitoring or specialized care
- **Risk Stratification:** Process of categorizing patients based on likelihood of complications
- **Early Warning Score (EWS):** Standardized scoring system to detect clinical deterioration
- **Enhanced Monitoring:** Increased frequency of observations and assessments

---

## 3. High-Risk Patient Categories

### 3.1 Clinical High-Risk Categories

| Category | Criteria | Risk Level |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **Critically Ill** | ICU admission, ventilator support, multiple organ dysfunction | Very High |
| **Cardiac High-Risk** | Acute MI, unstable angina, arrhythmias, heart failure | High |
| **Respiratory High-Risk** | Respiratory failure, COPD exacerbation, severe pneumonia | High |
| **Neurological High-Risk** | Stroke, altered consciousness, seizures | High |
| **Sepsis/Infection** | Sepsis, severe infections, multi-drug resistant organisms | High |
| **Post-Operative** | Major surgery, high-risk procedures | High |
| **Obstetric High-Risk** | Pre-eclampsia, placenta previa, previous C-section, multiple pregnancy | High |
| **Neonatal High-Risk** | Preterm (<37 weeks), low birth weight, NICU admission | High |

### 3.2 Vulnerable Population Categories

| Category | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| **Elderly** | Age >65 years with comorbidities |
| **Pediatric** | Age <5 years, especially neonates |
| **Immunocompromised** | HIV/AIDS, chemotherapy, transplant patients |
| **Psychiatric** | Suicidal ideation, violent behavior, severe mental illness |
| **Cognitively Impaired** | Dementia, delirium, intellectual disability |
| **Fall Risk** | Previous falls, mobility issues, medications affecting balance |
| **Pressure Ulcer Risk** | Immobility, incontinence, poor nutrition |

### 3.3 Procedure-Related High-Risk

| Procedure | Risk Factors |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Emergency Surgery** | All emergency operative cases |
| **Anesthesia Risk** | ASA Class III and above |
| **Blood Transfusion** | Requiring multiple transfusions |
| **Moderate/Deep Sedation** | All sedation procedures |
| **Restraint Use** | Patients requiring physical/chemical restraints |

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Identification Process

#### Step 1: Initial Risk Assessment at Admission
- **Responsible:** Admitting Physician / Nurse
- **Timeframe:** Within 4 hours of admission

**Assessment Tools:**
1. Complete medical history review
2. Physical examination findings
3. Laboratory and diagnostic results
4. Standardized risk assessment tools:
   - Fall Risk Assessment (Morse Fall Scale)
   - Pressure Ulcer Risk (Braden Scale)
   - Nutritional Risk (NRS-2002)
   - Suicide Risk Assessment (for psychiatric patients)

#### Step 2: Risk Categorization
- **Responsible:** Physician
- **Timeframe:** Immediate upon identification

**Documentation:**
1. Document in admission notes
2. Complete "High-Risk Patient Identification Form"
3. Flag in electronic medical record (if available)
4. Apply appropriate colored wristband/sticker

#### Step 3: Flagging and Communication
- **Responsible:** Nurse
- **Timeframe:** Within 1 hour of identification

**Visual Flagging System:**

| Risk Type | Flag Color | Placement |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Fall Risk | Yellow | Wristband + Bed |
| Pressure Ulcer Risk | Purple | Bed sticker |
| Infection/Isolation | Orange | Door + Chart |
| Allergy | Red | Wristband |
| Suicide/Harm Risk | Blue | Chart (discreet) |
| DNR/Advance Directive | Pink | Chart + Bed |
| VIP/Special Attention | White | Chart only |

**Communication:**
1. Verbal handover at shift change
2. Entry in nursing kardex
3. Whiteboard display (without compromising confidentiality)
4. Electronic alert in EMR

### 5.2 Enhanced Monitoring Protocol

#### Monitoring Frequency by Risk Level

| Risk Level | Vital Signs | Nursing Assessment | Physician Review |
|------------|-------------|--------------------| ----------------|
| Very High (ICU) | Continuous/Hourly | Every 2 hours | Every 4-6 hours |
| High | Every 4 hours | Every shift | Daily |
| Moderate | Every 6 hours | Every shift | As needed |

#### Early Warning Score (EWS) Monitoring

**Parameters Assessed:**
- Respiratory rate
- Oxygen saturation
- Supplemental oxygen
- Temperature
- Systolic blood pressure
- Heart rate
- Level of consciousness (AVPU)

**Score Interpretation and Response:**

| EWS Score | Risk | Response |
|-----------|------|----------|
| 0-4 | Low | Continue routine monitoring |
| 5-6 | Medium | Increase monitoring to 4-hourly, inform duty doctor |
| 7+ | High | Immediate medical review, consider ICU transfer |

### 5.3 Care Planning for High-Risk Patients

#### Step 1: Develop Individualized Care Plan
- **Responsible:** Multidisciplinary Team
- **Timeframe:** Within 24 hours of admission

**Care Plan Elements:**
1. Identified risks and their specific interventions
2. Monitoring parameters and frequency
3. Preventive measures
4. Escalation criteria
5. Team responsibilities
6. Patient/family education needs

#### Step 2: Implement Preventive Measures

**Fall Prevention:**
- Bed at lowest position
- Call bell within reach
- Non-slip footwear
- Adequate lighting
- Toileting schedule
- Medication review

**Pressure Ulcer Prevention:**
- Repositioning every 2 hours
- Pressure-relieving mattress
- Skin assessment daily
- Nutrition optimization
- Incontinence management

**Infection Prevention:**
- Appropriate isolation
- Hand hygiene compliance
- Device care bundles (CLABSI, CAUTI, VAP)
- Antimicrobial stewardship

#### Step 3: Daily Review
- **Responsible:** Treating Physician / Consultant
- **Timeframe:** Daily during rounds

**Review Elements:**
1. Current risk status
2. Response to interventions
3. New risks identified
4. Care plan modifications needed
5. Discharge readiness

### 5.4 Escalation Protocol

#### When to Escalate

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| EWS ≥7 | Immediate physician review, consider ICU |
| Rapid deterioration | Activate rapid response/MET call |
| New critical finding | Immediate consultant notification |
| Unresponsive to treatment | Specialist consultation |
| Family concern | Physician assessment within 1 hour |

#### Rapid Response/Medical Emergency Team (MET) Activation Criteria
- Acute change in heart rate (<40 or >140/min)
- Acute change in systolic BP (<90 mmHg)
- Acute change in respiratory rate (<8 or >28/min)
- Acute change in oxygen saturation (<90% on oxygen)
- Acute change in consciousness
- Staff concern about patient condition

### 5.5 Discharge Planning for High-Risk Patients

#### Pre-Discharge Assessment
1. All identified risks addressed or stable
2. Medications optimized
3. Follow-up appointments scheduled
4. Patient/family education completed
5. Home care needs identified
6. Caregiver capable and available
7. Equipment/supplies arranged

#### Discharge Documentation
1. Discharge summary with risk factors highlighted
2. Warning signs to watch for
3. When to seek emergency care
4. Follow-up schedule
5. Medication list with instructions
6. Contact numbers
