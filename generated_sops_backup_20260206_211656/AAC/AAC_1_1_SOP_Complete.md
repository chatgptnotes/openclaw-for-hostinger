## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To ensure 24x7 availability of emergency services and provide immediate, unhindered access to emergency care for all patients regardless of ability to pay, as mandated by regulatory requirements and NABH standards.

**Scope:** This SOP covers:
- Emergency department operations and availability
- Patient access procedures
- Staffing requirements
- Equipment and resource readiness
- Communication and coordination protocols

**Applicability:** 
- Emergency Department staff (doctors, nurses, technicians)
- Casualty Medical Officers (CMOs)
- Security personnel
- Reception and registration staff
- Ambulance services
- Hospital administration

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Emergency:** Any condition requiring immediate medical attention to prevent loss of life, limb, or organ
- **Triage:** Process of determining priority of patients' treatments based on severity of condition
- **Golden Hour:** Critical first hour after traumatic injury
- **Code Blue:** Cardiac/respiratory arrest emergency
- **MLC:** Medico-Legal Case

---

## 3. Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Emergency Department In-Charge | Overall ED operations, staffing, quality |
| Casualty Medical Officer (CMO) | Patient assessment, treatment initiation, admission decisions |
| Triage Nurse | Initial assessment, priority assignment |
| ED Nursing In-Charge | Nursing care, bed management, supplies |
| Security Officer | Access control, patient flow, safety |
| Registration Staff | Quick registration, documentation |
| Hospital Administrator (On-call) | Resource mobilization, escalation |

---

## 4. Policy Statement

Hope & Ayushman Hospitals provides emergency services 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. No patient shall be denied emergency care due to inability to pay. Initial stabilization and treatment shall be provided to all emergency patients before any financial considerations.

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Emergency Department Availability

#### Operating Hours
- **Open 24x7** - No closure at any time
- Minimum staffing maintained at all hours
- Specialist on-call coverage for all critical specialties

#### Minimum Staffing (Per Shift)

| Shift | CMO | Nurses | Technician | Support |
|-------|-----|--------|------------|---------|
| Morning (8AM-2PM) | 2 | 4 | 2 | 2 |
| Afternoon (2PM-8PM) | 2 | 4 | 2 | 2 |
| Night (8PM-8AM) | 1 | 3 | 1 | 1 |

#### On-Call Specialists (24x7)
- General Medicine
- General Surgery
- Orthopedics
- Obstetrics & Gynecology
- Pediatrics
- Anesthesiology
- Critical Care

### 5.2 Patient Access Procedure

#### Step 1: Arrival and Initial Contact
- **Timeframe:** Immediate (within 1 minute of arrival)
- **Responsible:** Security/Triage Nurse

**Actions:**
1. Security allows immediate entry - no barriers for emergency patients
2. Ambulance patients directed to dedicated bay
3. Walk-in emergencies guided to triage area
4. Triage nurse initiates quick assessment

#### Step 2: Triage Assessment
- **Timeframe:** Within 5 minutes of arrival
- **Responsible:** Triage Nurse

**Triage Categories (Color-Coded):**
| Category | Color | Criteria | Target Time to CMO |
|----------|-------|----------|-------------------|
| Immediate | RED | Life-threatening, requires immediate intervention | Immediate |
| Urgent | YELLOW | Serious, can wait up to 30 minutes | <30 minutes |
| Non-Urgent | GREEN | Minor, can wait up to 2 hours | <2 hours |
| Dead | BLACK | No signs of life | N/A |

#### Step 3: Registration
- **Timeframe:** Concurrent with or after stabilization
- **Responsible:** Registration Staff

**Actions:**
1. **Emergency patients:** Bedside registration after stabilization
2. **Basic information only initially:** Name, age, gender, address, contact
3. **Full registration:** After patient is stable
4. **MLC cases:** Inform police as per protocol

**Note:** Registration MUST NOT delay treatment

#### Step 4: Medical Assessment and Treatment
- **Timeframe:** As per triage category
- **Responsible:** CMO / Duty Doctor

**Actions:**
1. Systematic primary survey (ABCDE approach)
2. Initiate life-saving measures as needed
3. Order investigations (point-of-care where available)
4. Administer emergency medications
5. Document in Emergency Medical Record
6. Decide disposition: Admit / Observe / Discharge / Refer

#### Step 5: Disposition
- **Timeframe:** Within 4 hours for most cases
- **Responsible:** CMO

**Options:**
1. **Admission:** Coordinate with bed management
2. **Observation:** Move to ED observation area (max 24 hours)
3. **Discharge:** Provide advice, prescriptions, follow-up
4. **Referral:** Arrange safe transfer with documentation

### 5.3 Special Access Provisions

#### Medico-Legal Cases (MLC)
1. Provide treatment first
2. Inform police within 30 minutes
3. Document injuries precisely
4. Preserve evidence (clothing, bullets, etc.)
5. Complete MLC form

#### Brought Dead Cases
1. CMO must examine and certify
2. Inform police if suspicious
3. Document any external injuries
4. Guide family sensitively
5. Complete formalities per protocol

#### Unable-to-Pay Patients
1. Provide full emergency care
2. Document under "Poor Patient Fund" or charity category
3. Social worker to arrange assistance
4. Never deny or delay care due to payment issues

### 5.4 Infrastructure Requirements

#### Physical Setup
- Clearly marked Emergency entrance with signage
- Separate ambulance bay
- Wheelchair and stretcher availability at entrance
- Ramp access for disabled patients
- Dedicated examination rooms (minimum 4)
- Resuscitation bay with 2 beds
- Observation area with 6 beds
- Separate pediatric emergency area
- Isolation room for infectious patients

#### Equipment Checklist (Daily Verification)

## 6. Communication Protocols

### Internal Communication
- **Code Blue:** Overhead announcement + pager alert
- **Mass Casualty:** Activate disaster code
- **Bed requests:** Direct line to bed management
- **Specialist consultation:** On-call contact list

### External Communication
- **Ambulance services:** Direct hotline display
- **Police (MLC):** Emergency contact numbers
- **Referral hospitals:** Pre-arranged agreements
- **Blood bank:** 24x7 contact

## 10. Escalation Protocol

| Situation | Escalate To | Timeline |
|-----------|-------------|----------|

| Equipment failure | Biomedical Engineering | Immediate |
| Security issue | Security In-Charge → Administrator | Immediate |
| Mass casualty (>5 critical) | Activate Disaster Plan | Immediate |
