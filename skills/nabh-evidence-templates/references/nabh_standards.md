# NABH Standards Reference (5th Edition)

## Overview
National Accreditation Board for Hospitals & Healthcare Providers (NABH) standards provide a framework for hospital quality and patient safety. This reference covers the key standards and evidence requirements.

## Chapter Structure

### COP - Continuum of Care
**Purpose:** Ensure seamless patient care across all departments and services

**Key Standards:**
- **COP.1**: Hospital provides care across the continuum
- **COP.2**: Care is coordinated among caregivers and departments
- **COP.3**: Referrals and follow-up are coordinated

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Patient flow charts and pathways
- Inter-departmental coordination protocols
- Referral and discharge policies
- Communication logs between departments
- Patient handover documentation

### ASC - Assessment of Patients
**Purpose:** Standardize patient assessment processes across all care areas

**Key Standards:**
- **ASC.1**: All patients undergo initial assessment
- **ASC.2**: Patients undergo assessment based on their needs
- **ASC.3**: Qualified individuals conduct assessments
- **ASC.4**: Patients are reassessed when warranted

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Assessment forms and checklists
- Competency records of assessment staff
- Reassessment protocols and triggers
- Assessment timeframes documentation
- Quality reviews of assessments

### CCC - Care of Patients  
**Purpose:** Ensure uniform, effective, and safe patient care delivery

**Key Standards:**
- **CCC.1**: Hospital develops care plans for all patients
- **CCC.2**: Care is provided according to established care plans
- **CCC.3**: Care is provided by qualified individuals
- **CCC.4**: Each patient's care is reviewed regularly

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Standardized care plans and protocols
- Staff qualification and competency records  
- Care plan review documentation
- Patient progress monitoring systems
- Care modification documentation

### MMU - Medication Management and Use
**Purpose:** Ensure safe and effective medication management throughout the hospital

**Key Standards:**
- **MMU.1**: Hospital manages medication availability
- **MMU.2**: Medication orders are clearly and accurately communicated
- **MMU.3**: Medication preparation and dispensing is safe
- **MMU.4**: Safe medication administration practices
- **MMU.5**: Monitoring for medication effects

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Medication procurement and storage policies
- Prescription and order verification systems
- High-alert medication protocols
- Medication error reporting and analysis
- Adverse drug reaction monitoring
- Controlled drug management systems

### PSG - Patient Safety Goals
**Purpose:** Implement evidence-based patient safety practices

**Key Goals:**
- **PSG.1**: Improve accuracy of patient identification
- **PSG.2**: Improve effectiveness of communication among caregivers
- **PSG.3**: Improve safety of high-alert medications
- **PSG.4**: Eliminate wrong-site, wrong-patient surgeries
- **PSG.5**: Reduce risk of healthcare-associated infections
- **PSG.6**: Reduce risk of patient harm from falls

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Patient identification protocols and verification
- Critical value communication systems
- High-alert medication safety measures
- Surgical safety checklists and time-outs
- Infection control surveillance data
- Fall risk assessment and prevention programs

### IPC - Infection Prevention and Control
**Purpose:** Minimize healthcare-associated infection risks

**Key Standards:**
- **IPC.1**: Hospital has an infection prevention program
- **IPC.2**: Hand hygiene program implementation
- **IPC.3**: Surveillance of healthcare-associated infections
- **IPC.4**: Isolation precautions when indicated
- **IPC.5**: Equipment and device management

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Infection control committee minutes
- Hand hygiene compliance data
- Infection surveillance reports
- Isolation protocol documentation
- Equipment sterilization and maintenance records
- Environmental monitoring data

### FMS - Facility Management and Safety
**Purpose:** Provide safe, functional, and supportive environment

**Key Standards:**
- **FMS.1**: Hospital plans and implements safety management
- **FMS.2**: Hospital manages risks and maintains safe environment
- **FMS.3**: Utility systems are maintained
- **FMS.4**: Medical equipment management program
- **FMS.5**: Inventory management

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Safety management plans and policies
- Risk assessment documentation
- Utility system maintenance logs
- Medical equipment calibration records
- Fire safety and emergency response procedures
- Hazardous material management protocols

### HMS - Human Resource Management
**Purpose:** Ensure qualified, competent healthcare workforce

**Key Standards:**
- **HMS.1**: Hospital plans human resource needs
- **HMS.2**: Staff qualifications match job requirements
- **HMS.3**: Staff receive orientation and education
- **HMS.4**: Ongoing education and competency assessment
- **HMS.5**: Health and safety of staff

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Staffing plans and workload analysis
- Credential verification and qualification documents
- Orientation and training programs
- Competency assessment records
- Staff health and vaccination records
- Continuing education documentation

### IMS - Information Management System
**Purpose:** Effective information management supporting patient care

**Key Standards:**
- **IMS.1**: Information needs are identified
- **IMS.2**: Information systems support patient care
- **IMS.3**: Patient record management
- **IMS.4**: Health information privacy and confidentiality
- **IMS.5**: Data and information security

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Information management policies
- Electronic health record systems documentation
- Patient record audit reports
- Privacy and confidentiality training
- Data security and backup procedures
- Information access control systems

### QI - Quality Improvement
**Purpose:** Systematic quality improvement throughout the organization

**Key Standards:**
- **QI.1**: Hospital leaders set improvement priorities
- **QI.2**: Data collection and analysis for improvement
- **QI.3**: Analysis of improvement opportunities
- **QI.4**: Implementation of improvements
- **QI.5**: Hospital leadership demonstrates commitment to quality

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Quality committee meeting minutes
- Quality indicators and dashboard data
- Improvement project documentation
- Root cause analysis reports
- Patient satisfaction survey results
- Clinical quality outcome measurements

## Evidence Documentation Standards

### Essential Elements for All Evidence
1. **Identification**: Clear labeling with NABH standard reference
2. **Ownership**: Designated responsible person or department
3. **Currency**: Regular review and update schedules
4. **Traceability**: Version control and change documentation
5. **Accessibility**: Easy retrieval for audit purposes

### Documentation Hierarchy
1. **Primary Documents**: Policies, procedures, protocols
2. **Implementation Records**: Training logs, competency assessments
3. **Performance Data**: Metrics, indicators, surveillance results
4. **Review Documentation**: Audit reports, quality assessments
5. **Improvement Evidence**: Action plans, corrective measures

### Quality Requirements
- Evidence must be current and reflect actual practices
- Documentation should be comprehensive yet concise
- All evidence must be easily verifiable during assessment
- Cross-referencing between related standards is essential
- Regular internal audits to validate evidence accuracy

## Assessment Preparation
- Organize evidence by chapter and standard
- Ensure all supporting documentation is readily available
- Prepare staff for interviews and demonstrations
- Conduct mock assessments to identify gaps
- Maintain evidence database for ongoing compliance monitoring
