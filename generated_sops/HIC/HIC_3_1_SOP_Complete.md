## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To establish baseline infection prevention practices that apply to ALL patients in ALL healthcare settings, regardless of suspected or confirmed infection status, to prevent transmission of infectious agents during patient care.

**Scope:** Standard Precautions apply to:
- Blood
- All body fluids, secretions, and excretions (except sweat)
- Non-intact skin
- Mucous membranes

**Applicability:** All healthcare workers, students, trainees, volunteers, and visitors who have direct or indirect contact with patients or patient care environments.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Standard Precautions:** Minimum infection prevention practices that apply to all patient care
- **PPE:** Personal Protective Equipment
- **Sharps:** Needles, scalpels, broken glass, or any object that can penetrate skin
- **Respiratory Hygiene/Cough Etiquette:** Measures to contain respiratory secretions
- **Safe Injection Practices:** Practices to prevent transmission of bloodborne pathogens

## 4. Components of Standard Precautions

### 4.1 Hand Hygiene
*See detailed protocol: SOP-HIC-2.1*

- Perform hand hygiene before and after every patient contact
- Use alcohol-based hand rub (ABHR) for routine decontamination
- Wash with soap and water when hands are visibly soiled
- Hand hygiene after removing gloves

### 4.2 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

#### Gloves
- Touching blood, body fluids, secretions, excretions
- Touching mucous membranes or non-intact skin
- Handling contaminated items or surfaces
- Performing venipuncture, IV access, or invasive procedures

1. Perform hand hygiene before donning gloves
2. Select appropriate size and type
3. Change gloves between patients
4. Change gloves between dirty and clean procedures on same patient
5. Remove gloves properly (peel off from wrist, avoiding touch of outer surface)
6. Perform hand hygiene immediately after removal
7. Never wash or reuse gloves

#### Gown/Apron
- Procedures likely to generate splashes/sprays of blood or body fluids
- Direct contact with patient who has uncontained secretions
- Entering isolation rooms

1. Secure at neck and waist
2. Remove by pulling from shoulders
3. Roll inward (contaminated side in)
4. Discard in appropriate container
5. Perform hand hygiene

#### Mask
- During procedures likely to generate splashes/sprays to face
- Patient care involving respiratory secretions
- During sterile procedures
- When patient is on droplet precautions

- Surgical mask: Standard procedures, droplet precautions
- N95 respirator: Airborne precautions, aerosol-generating procedures

1. Secure upper and lower ties/straps
2. Mold nose piece to nose
3. Do not touch front of mask while wearing
4. Remove by touching only ties/straps
5. Discard properly, hand hygiene

#### Eye Protection/Face Shield
- Procedures likely to generate splashes to eyes
- Caring for patients with respiratory infections
- During aerosol-generating procedures

### 4.3 Respiratory Hygiene/Cough Etiquette

1. Cover mouth/nose when coughing or sneezing
2. Use tissue, dispose immediately
3. Perform hand hygiene after contact with respiratory secretions
4. Wear surgical mask if coughing
5. Maintain distance (>1 meter) from others

- Provide tissues and masks at entry points
- Display visual alerts (posters)
- Provide hand hygiene supplies
- Maintain spatial separation in waiting areas

### 4.4 Safe Injection Practices

1. Use aseptic technique when preparing and administering injections
2. Use single-dose vials whenever possible
3. Never use same syringe for multiple patients
4. Never enter multi-dose vial with used syringe/needle
5. Insulin pens are single-patient use only

1. Clean hands and work surface
2. Inspect medication for contamination/expiry
3. Use sterile syringe and needle for each injection
4. Prepare in clean designated area
5. Discard unused medication from single-dose vials

### 4.5 Sharps Safety

1. Never recap used needles
2. Use needle-free systems when available
3. Use safety-engineered devices
4. Dispose immediately after use in sharps container
5. Never bend, break, or manipulate used sharps

- Puncture-resistant
- Located at point of use
- Visible fill line (do not overfill)
- Replace when 3/4 full
- Do not reach into container

1. Allow wound to bleed, wash with soap and water
2. Report immediately to supervisor
3. Seek medical evaluation within 1-2 hours
4. Complete incident report
5. Follow post-exposure prophylaxis protocol

### 4.6 Environmental Cleaning

1. Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces
2. Use hospital-approved disinfectants
3. Follow contact time recommendations
4. Clean from clean to dirty areas
5. Change cleaning solutions regularly


| Area | Cleaning Frequency |
|------|-------------------|
| Patient room | At least daily + terminal cleaning |
| High-touch surfaces | At least twice daily |
| Spills | Immediately |
| Equipment (shared) | Between patients |

**High-Touch Surfaces:**
- Bed rails
- Call buttons
- Door handles
- Light switches
- Tray tables
- IV poles
- Monitor buttons

### 4.7 Patient Care Equipment

**Single-Use Items:**
- Use once and discard
- Never reprocess items labeled single-use

1. Clean and disinfect between patients
2. Follow manufacturer's reprocessing instructions
3. Allow to dry completely before use/storage
4. Critical items: Sterilize
5. Semi-critical items: High-level disinfection
6. Non-critical items: Low-level disinfection

### 4.8 Linen Handling

1. Handle with minimum agitation
2. Do not shake or sort in patient areas
3. Bag at point of use
4. Use leak-resistant bags for heavily soiled linen
5. Transport in closed carts

1. Store in clean, enclosed area
2. Handle with clean hands
3. Do not store excess in patient rooms
4. Protect from contamination during transport

### 4.9 Waste Management


| Waste Type | Container | Color Code |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| General waste | Bin with lid | Black |
| Infectious waste | Biohazard container | Yellow |
| Sharps | Puncture-proof | Blue/White translucent |
| Pathological | Biohazard container | Yellow |
| Chemical waste | As per MSDS | As specified |
| Cytotoxic | Cytotoxic container | Purple/Yellow |
| Recyclable | Separate container | Green/Blue |

## 5. Specific Procedures

### 5.1 Blood/Body Fluid Spill Management

**Small Spill (<10 ml):**
1. Wear gloves
2. Wipe with absorbent material
3. Clean with detergent
4. Disinfect with sodium hypochlorite (1%)
5. Dispose in yellow bag
6. Hand hygiene

**Large Spill (>10 ml):**
1. Evacuate area if needed
2. Wear full PPE (gloves, gown, mask, eye protection)
3. Cover spill with absorbent material
4. Pour sodium hypochlorite 1% (10,000 ppm) around and over spill
5. Leave for 10 minutes
6. Absorb with disposable cloths from periphery to center
7. Clean area with detergent and water
8. Disinfect with sodium hypochlorite
9. Dispose all materials as infectious waste
10. Remove PPE, hand hygiene
11. Document incident

### 5.2 PPE Donning Sequence
1. Hand hygiene
2. Gown
3. Mask/Respirator
4. Eye protection
5. Gloves

### 5.3 PPE Doffing Sequence (Safe Removal)
1. Gloves (outside is contaminated)
2. Hand hygiene
3. Gown (outside is contaminated)
4. Hand hygiene
5. Eye protection (outside is contaminated)
6. Hand hygiene
7. Mask (front is contaminated)
8. Hand hygiene
