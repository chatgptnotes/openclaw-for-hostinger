## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To establish standardized hand hygiene practices across all healthcare settings to prevent healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) and ensure patient and staff safety through compliance with WHO "My Five Moments for Hand Hygiene" guidelines.

**Scope:** This SOP applies to all clinical and non-clinical staff, visitors, contractors, and anyone who enters patient care areas within Hope & Ayushman Hospitals.

- All doctors, nurses, and allied health professionals
- Housekeeping and support staff
- Laboratory and radiology personnel
- Administrative staff entering clinical areas
- Visitors and patient attendants

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Hand Hygiene:** General term referring to handwashing, antiseptic hand wash, antiseptic hand rub, or surgical hand antisepsis
- **ABHR:** Alcohol-Based Hand Rub (60-95% alcohol content)
- **HAI:** Healthcare-Associated Infection
- **WHO:** World Health Organization
- **Five Moments:** WHO's defined critical moments for hand hygiene

## 4. Policy Statement

Hand hygiene is the single most effective measure to prevent healthcare-associated infections. All personnel must perform hand hygiene at the WHO-defined "Five Moments" and whenever hands are visibly soiled.

---

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 WHO Five Moments for Hand Hygiene

All staff must perform hand hygiene at these critical moments:

1. **Before Touching a Patient** - Protect the patient from harmful germs from your hands
2. **Before Clean/Aseptic Procedure** - Protect the patient against harmful germs entering their body
3. **After Body Fluid Exposure Risk** - Protect yourself and the healthcare environment from harmful patient germs
4. **After Touching a Patient** - Protect yourself and the healthcare environment from harmful patient germs
5. **After Touching Patient Surroundings** - Protect yourself and the healthcare environment from harmful patient germs

### 5.2 Methods of Hand Hygiene

#### A. Alcohol-Based Hand Rub (ABHR) - Preferred Method
**Duration:** 20-30 seconds
**Use when:** Hands are NOT visibly soiled

1. Apply a palmful (3-5 ml) of ABHR to cupped hand
2. Rub hands palm to palm
3. Rub right palm over left dorsum with interlaced fingers and vice versa
4. Rub palm to palm with fingers interlaced
5. Rub backs of fingers to opposing palms with fingers interlocked
6. Rotational rubbing of left thumb clasped in right palm and vice versa
7. Rotational rubbing, backwards and forwards with clasped fingers of right hand in left palm and vice versa
8. Allow to dry completely - do not wipe

#### B. Handwashing with Soap and Water
**Duration:** 40-60 seconds
**Use when:** Hands are visibly dirty/soiled, after using toilet, after caring for C. difficile or Norovirus patients, after removing gloves

1. Wet hands with water
2. Apply enough soap to cover all hand surfaces
3. Rub hands palm to palm
4. Rub right palm over left dorsum with interlaced fingers and vice versa
5. Rub palm to palm with fingers interlaced
6. Rub backs of fingers to opposing palms with fingers interlocked
7. Rotational rubbing of left thumb clasped in right palm and vice versa
8. Rotational rubbing of clasped fingers in palm
9. Rinse hands with water
10. Dry thoroughly with single-use towel
11. Use towel to turn off faucet

### 5.3 Special Situations

#### Surgical Hand Antisepsis
- Perform before all surgical procedures
- Duration: 2-5 minutes with antimicrobial soap OR 2 applications of ABHR
- Follow surgical hand scrub protocol (SOP-HIC-4.1)

#### Contact Precautions
- Always wash with soap and water when caring for patients with:
  - Clostridioides difficile infection
  - Norovirus gastroenteritis
  - Suspected or confirmed spore-forming organisms

#### Glove Use
- Hand hygiene before donning gloves
- Hand hygiene immediately after removing gloves
- Gloves do NOT replace hand hygiene

## 6. Infrastructure Requirements

### 6.1 ABHR Dispensers
- Located at point of care (within arm's reach)
- At all room entrances
- In corridors at 10-meter intervals
- Near nursing stations
- Personal pocket bottles available for mobile staff

### 6.2 Handwashing Sinks
- Elbow-operated or sensor taps preferred
- Liquid soap in wall-mounted dispensers
- Single-use paper towels
- Hand hygiene poster at eye level
- Sink cleaning schedule maintained

### 6.3 Supplies
- ABHR (60-95% alcohol, WHO-formulation preferred)
- Antimicrobial liquid soap
- Plain liquid soap (for general handwashing)
- Paper towels
- Hand moisturizers (non-petroleum based)

## 8. Monitoring and Compliance

### 8.1 Compliance Auditing

| Parameter | Target | Frequency | Method |
|-----------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Overall Compliance Rate | ≥80% | Monthly | Direct observation |
| ABHR Consumption | 20ml/patient-day (minimum) | Monthly | Product usage tracking |
| Five Moments Compliance | ≥80% per moment | Monthly | WHO audit tool |
| Staff Training Completion | 100% | Quarterly | Training records |

### 8.2 Audit Process
1. ICN conducts unannounced observations using WHO Hand Hygiene Observation Form
2. Minimum 200 observations per month hospital-wide
3. Results stratified by unit, staff category, and moment
4. Feedback provided to units within 7 days
5. Action plans for units below target

### 8.3 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- **Hand Hygiene Compliance Rate**: Target ≥80%
- **ABHR Consumption per Patient-Day**: Target ≥20ml
- **HAI Rate**: Monitored for correlation with compliance
- **Staff Training Rate**: 100% within 30 days of joining

2. **Repeated Non-Compliance:** Written counseling by Unit Manager
3. **Persistent Non-Compliance:** Report to ICO for intervention
4. **Willful Violation:** Escalation to HR and Medical Superintendent
