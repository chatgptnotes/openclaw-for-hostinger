## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To establish a comprehensive patient safety program that creates a culture of safety, implements proactive risk reduction strategies, promotes incident reporting without fear, and drives continuous improvement in patient safety across the organization.

**Scope:** This program encompasses all aspects of patient care including:
- Patient Safety Goals implementation
- Incident reporting and management
- Risk identification and mitigation
- Safety culture development
- Staff training and competency
- Performance monitoring and improvement

**Applicability:** All clinical and non-clinical staff involved directly or indirectly in patient care.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Patient Safety:** Freedom from accidental injury due to medical care
- **Adverse Event:** Unintended injury caused by medical management rather than the disease process
- **Near Miss:** Event that could have caused harm but did not reach the patient
- **Sentinel Event:** Unexpected occurrence involving death or serious physical or psychological injury
- **RCA:** Root Cause Analysis
- **FMEA:** Failure Mode and Effects Analysis

## 6. Incident Reporting System

### 6.1 What to Report

**Must Report (Within 1 Hour):**
- Deaths related to treatment
- Serious adverse events
- Medication errors causing harm
- Wrong patient/site/procedure
- Falls with injury
- Blood transfusion reactions
- Retained surgical items
- Device/equipment failures causing harm

**Should Report (Within 24 Hours):**
- Near misses
- Medication errors without harm
- Falls without injury
- Documentation errors
- Communication breakdowns
- Process failures
- Equipment malfunctions

### 6.2 How to Report

1. **Incident Reporting Form** (Paper/Electronic)
2. **Confidential Hotline** (available 24x7)
3. **Email** to patientsafety@hospital.com
4. **Safety Champion** in the unit
5. **Anonymous Box** (for sensitive reports)

**Non-Punitive Policy:**
- Reporting is encouraged and protected
- No punitive action for honest errors
- Focus on system improvement, not blame
- Confidentiality maintained
- Recognition for active reporters

### 6.3 Incident Classification

| Severity | Definition | Response Time |
|----------|------------|---------------|
| **Sentinel** | Death or permanent harm | Immediate |
| **Severe** | Temporary harm requiring intervention | <24 hours |
| **Moderate** | Additional monitoring/treatment | <48 hours |
| **Minor** | No harm, minimal intervention | <72 hours |
| **Near Miss** | Did not reach patient | <1 week |

### 6.4 Incident Investigation

1. Immediate actions to prevent recurrence
2. Preliminary report within 24 hours
3. Initial investigation by unit manager
4. Classification and escalation if needed

**For Serious/Sentinel Events:**
1. Notify Patient Safety Officer immediately
2. Preserve all evidence
3. Conduct Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
4. Complete within 45 days
5. Present to Patient Safety Committee
6. Implement corrective actions
7. Monitor effectiveness

## 7. Root Cause Analysis (RCA) Process

### 7.1 When to Conduct RCA

- All sentinel events
- Serious adverse events
- Recurring incidents (pattern)
- High-risk near misses

### 7.2 RCA Methodology

**Step 1: Event Summary**
- What happened? (factual description)
- When and where?
- Who was involved?

**Step 2: Data Collection**
- Medical records
- Staff interviews
- Equipment checks
- Environment assessment
- Policies/procedures review

**Step 3: Cause Analysis**
- Use "5 Whys" technique
- Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram
- Identify contributing factors:
  - Human factors
  - Process/system factors
  - Equipment factors
  - Environmental factors
  - Organizational factors

**Step 4: Root Cause Identification**
- Identify underlying system failures
- Determine correctable causes
- Prioritize based on impact and feasibility

**Step 5: Corrective Action Plan**
- Specific, measurable actions
- Responsible persons assigned
- Timeline for completion
- Resources needed

**Step 6: Implementation and Monitoring**
- Execute action plan
- Monitor effectiveness
- Close loop with reporting

## 8. Proactive Risk Management

### 8.1 Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA)

- New processes before implementation
- High-risk processes
- Processes with known vulnerabilities
- After significant changes

1. Define the process
2. Identify potential failure modes
3. Determine effects of each failure
4. Rate Severity, Occurrence, Detection (1-10)
5. Calculate Risk Priority Number (RPN)
6. Prioritize and address high RPN items
7. Re-evaluate after improvements

### 8.2 Safety Rounds

- Monthly by leadership team
- Visit all clinical areas over 3 months
- Identify hazards and safety concerns
- Engage staff in safety discussions
- Document findings and actions

- Weekly by department heads
- Focus on near misses and concerns
- Equipment and environment checks
- Staff feedback collection

## 9. Safety Culture

### 9.1 Just Culture Framework

| Behavior | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Human Error | Console the staff member, fix the system |
| At-Risk Behavior | Coach the staff, remove incentives for risk |
| Reckless Behavior | Disciplinary action (rare) |

### 9.2 Safety Culture Activities

- **Safety Moments:** Begin meetings with safety discussion
- **Good Catch Awards:** Recognize near-miss reporters
- **Safety Newsletters:** Monthly safety updates
- **Safety Week:** Annual awareness campaign
- **Speak Up Campaign:** Encourage questioning
- **Safety Culture Survey:** Annual (target: positive culture ≥70%)
