## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To ensure accurate patient identification before medication administration using at least two patient identifiers, preventing medication errors and enhancing patient safety as per NABH Patient Safety Goal #1.

**Scope:** This SOP applies to all medication administration scenarios including:
- Oral medications
- Injectable medications (IV, IM, SC)
- Infusions
- Blood products
- Contrast media
- High-alert medications

- All registered nurses and nursing staff
- Physicians administering medications
- Pharmacists during dispensing verification
- Technicians involved in medication delivery

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Patient Identifier:** Information specific to a patient that can be used to correctly match the patient to the medication
- **Two Patient Identifiers:** Minimum of two independent pieces of identifying information
- **UHID:** Unique Hospital Identification Number
- **High-Alert Medications:** Medications that bear heightened risk of causing significant patient harm
- **Barcode Medication Administration (BCMA):** Technology-assisted verification system

---

## 3. Accepted Patient Identifiers

### Primary Identifiers (Use ANY TWO)

| Identifier | Description | Reliability |
|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Patient Full Name | As registered in medical records | High |
| UHID Number | Unique Hospital ID on wristband | Highest |
| Date of Birth | Day/Month/Year | High |
| Father's/Spouse's Name | As registered | Medium |
| Mobile Number | Registered contact number | Medium |

### NOT Acceptable as Identifiers
- ❌ Room number or bed number
- ❌ Location (ICU Bed 3)
- ❌ Diagnosis or condition
- ❌ Physical appearance alone

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|

---

## 5. Policy Statement

Every patient must be positively identified using a minimum of two patient identifiers before any medication is administered. The room or bed number shall NEVER be used as an identifier. Staff must actively verify identity by asking the patient to state their identifiers, not by asking "Are you Mr. X?"

---

## 6. Procedure

### 6.1 Standard Identification Process

#### Step 1: Approach the Patient
- **Responsible:** Nurse/Administrator
- **Timeframe:** Before every medication administration

1. Carry medication, MAR (Medication Administration Record), and patient chart
2. Approach patient with medication tray/trolley
3. Introduce yourself if first interaction
4. Inform patient you will be giving medication

#### Step 2: Active Identification (Ask Patient to State)
- **Responsible:** Nurse
- **Script:** "For your safety, please tell me your full name and date of birth"

- ❌ "Are you Mr. Ramesh Kumar?"
- ❌ "You are Bed 5, right?"

- ✅ "Please tell me your full name"
- ✅ "What is your date of birth?"
- ✅ "What is your hospital ID number?"

#### Step 3: Verify Against Wristband
- **Responsible:** Nurse
- **Timeframe:** Every administration

1. Check patient's wristband is intact and legible
2. Match UHID on wristband with MAR
3. Match name on wristband with MAR
4. Ensure wristband is on correct patient (not detached)

- Missing → Replace BEFORE giving medication
- Damaged/illegible → Replace BEFORE giving medication
- Wrong information → Investigate and correct

#### Step 4: Verify Against MAR/Prescription
- **Responsible:** Nurse

- [ ] Patient name matches
- [ ] UHID matches
- [ ] Medication name is correct
- [ ] Dose is correct
- [ ] Route is correct
- [ ] Time is correct
- [ ] No documented allergies to this medication

#### Step 5: Administer Medication
- **Responsible:** Nurse

- Two identifiers confirmed by patient statement
- Wristband verification complete
- MAR verification complete
- Right medication, dose, route, time confirmed

#### Step 6: Document
- **Responsible:** Nurse
- **Timeframe:** Immediately after administration

- Medication given
- Dose, route, time
- Patient response (if applicable)
- Any adverse reaction
- Signature/initials

### 6.2 Special Situations

#### Unconscious/Unresponsive Patients
1. Use wristband verification (primary)
2. Verify with family member/attendant present
3. Cross-check with patient photo in EMR (if available)
4. Confirm with colleague if any doubt
5. Document the verification method used

#### Pediatric Patients
1. Verify with parent/guardian
2. Check wristband (on ankle for infants)
3. Parent to state child's name and DOB
4. Never rely on parent recognition alone

#### Patients Without Wristband
1. **STOP** - Do not administer medication
2. Verify identity through alternative methods (attendant, ID card, photo)
3. Apply new wristband with proper verification
4. Document incident
5. Then proceed with medication

#### Confused/Psychiatric Patients
1. Use wristband as primary identifier
2. Verify with attendant/caregiver
3. Cross-reference with photo in medical record
4. Document verification method

#### Emergency Situations
1. If patient identity unknown: "Unknown Male/Female" with temporary ID
2. Document circumstances
3. Update records once identity confirmed
4. Never skip verification even in emergencies

### 6.3 Technology-Assisted Verification (Where Available)

#### Barcode Scanning
1. Scan patient wristband barcode
2. Scan medication barcode
3. System confirms match or alerts mismatch
4. Proceed only on confirmation
5. Manual backup if system down

## 7. High-Alert Medication - Double Verification

For high-alert medications, TWO STAFF members must independently verify:

### High-Alert Medications List
- Insulin (all types)
- Anticoagulants (Heparin, Warfarin, Enoxaparin)
- Opioids (Morphine, Fentanyl, Pethidine)
- Potassium Chloride (concentrated)
- Chemotherapy agents
- Neuromuscular blocking agents
- Blood and blood products

### Double-Check Process
1. First nurse verifies patient identity (2 identifiers)
2. Second nurse independently verifies patient identity
3. Both verify medication against MAR
4. Both sign the MAR
5. One nurse administers

## 8. Wristband Management

### Wristband Information (Minimum)
- Patient Full Name
- UHID
- Age/DOB
- Gender
- Allergy status (color-coded if allergies present)

### Color Coding
| Color | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| White | Standard patient |
| Red | Known allergies |
| Yellow | Fall risk |
| Purple | DNR (if applicable) |

### Wristband Integrity Checks
- Check at every shift handover
- Check before every medication
- Replace if damaged, soiled, or illegible
- Document replacement

## 11. Non-Compliance and Error Management

### If Verification Not Done
1. Stop medication administration
2. Complete verification process
3. Report incident to Ward In-Charge
4. Document in incident reporting system
5. Coaching/counseling for staff

### If Wrong Patient Error Occurs
1. Stop administration immediately
2. Assess patient for adverse effects
3. Notify physician immediately
4. Inform patient/family
5. Complete incident report
6. Root cause analysis
7. Implement corrective actions
