## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To establish standardized emergency care protocols ensuring evidence-based, timely, and effective treatment for common emergency presentations at Hope & Ayushman Hospitals.

**Scope:** This SOP covers:
- Standardized treatment protocols for emergency conditions
- Clinical decision-making guidelines
- Medication administration protocols
- Monitoring and assessment procedures
- Discharge and referral criteria

**Applicable to:**
- Emergency physicians
- Casualty Medical Officers (CMOs)
- Emergency department nurses
- Paramedical staff
- Specialist consultants

## 2. Definitions

- **Emergency Protocol:** Standardized approach for specific emergency conditions
- **STEMI:** ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction
- **Sepsis:** Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by infection
- **TIA:** Transient Ischemic Attack
- **DKA:** Diabetic Ketoacidosis
- **COPD:** Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

## 5. Common Emergency Protocols

### 5.1 Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)

#### Initial Assessment (Within 10 minutes)
1. **12-lead ECG** within 10 minutes of arrival
2. **Vital signs** including pulse oximetry
3. **Brief history** focusing on chest pain characteristics
4. **Physical examination** with focus on cardiovascular system

#### STEMI Protocol (Door-to-Balloon ≤90 minutes)
**Inclusion Criteria:**
- Chest pain >30 minutes
- ST elevation ≥1mm in ≥2 contiguous leads
- New LBBB with clinical presentation

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Aspirin 325mg** chewed (unless contraindicated)
2. **Clopidogrel 600mg** loading dose
3. **Atorvastatin 80mg** 
4. **Metoprolol 25mg BD** (if no contraindications)
5. **Heparin** as per weight-based protocol

**Laboratory Tests (STAT):**
- Troponin I
- Complete Blood Count
- PT/INR, aPTT
- Creatinine, electrolytes
- Lipid profile

**Cardiac Catheterization Criteria:**
- All STEMI patients <12 hours from onset
- High-risk NSTEMI patients
- Cardiogenic shock

#### NSTEMI/Unstable Angina Protocol
**Risk Stratification (TIMI Score):**

| Risk Factor | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| Age ≥65 years | 1 |
| ≥3 CAD risk factors | 1 |
| Known CAD | 1 |
| ASA use in past 7 days | 1 |
| Severe anginal symptoms | 1 |
| ST changes ≥0.5mm | 1 |
| Elevated cardiac markers | 1 |

**Treatment Based on TIMI Score:**
- Score 0-2: Conservative management
- Score 3-4: Early invasive strategy within 24-48 hours
- Score 5-7: Urgent invasive strategy within 24 hours

### 5.2 Sepsis Protocol

#### Early Recognition (qSOFA Criteria)
Any 2 of the following:
- Respiratory rate ≥22/min
- Altered mentation (GCS <15)
- Systolic BP ≤100 mmHg

#### Sepsis Bundle (Complete within 3 hours)
1. **Blood cultures** before antibiotics (minimum 2 sets)
2. **Serum lactate** measurement
3. **Broad-spectrum antibiotics** within 1 hour
4. **Fluid resuscitation** 30ml/kg crystalloid if hypotensive

#### Septic Shock Protocol
**Additional measures within 6 hours:**
1. **Vasopressors** if hypotensive despite fluids
2. **Repeat lactate** if initially elevated
3. **Central venous pressure** monitoring
4. **ScvO2 monitoring** if available

**Antibiotic Guidelines:**

| Source | First-line Antibiotic | Duration |
|--------|--------------------|----------|
| Community-acquired pneumonia | Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin | 5-7 days |
| Healthcare-associated | Piperacillin-Tazobactam | 7-10 days |
| Intra-abdominal | Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole | 7-14 days |
| Urinary tract | Ceftriaxone | 7-10 days |
| Skin/soft tissue | Clindamycin or Vancomycin | 7-10 days |

### 5.3 Stroke Protocol

#### Acute Stroke Assessment (Within 25 minutes)
1. **Time last seen normal** establishment
2. **NIHSS score** assessment
3. **Blood glucose** check
4. **CT brain** without contrast

#### Inclusion Criteria for IV tPA
- Age ≥18 years
- Clinical diagnosis of ischemic stroke
- Onset <4.5 hours (or <3 hours if >80 years)
- NIHSS score ≥4

#### Exclusion Criteria for tPA
- Recent surgery (<14 days)
- Recent stroke (<3 months)
- Active bleeding
- Platelet count <100,000
- INR >1.7
- Blood pressure >185/110 mmHg

#### tPA Administration Protocol
**Pre-treatment:**
- Obtain informed consent
- Ensure CT shows no hemorrhage
- Control blood pressure <185/110

**Dosing:**
- 0.9 mg/kg (maximum 90mg)
- 10% as bolus over 1 minute
- Remainder over 60 minutes

**Monitoring:**
- Neurological checks q15 min × 2 hours
- Blood pressure q15 min × 2 hours
- Hold antiplatelets/anticoagulants × 24 hours

### 5.4 Anaphylaxis Protocol

#### Immediate Management (Within 5 minutes)
1. **Epinephrine 0.3-0.5mg IM** (anterolateral thigh)
2. **Remove trigger** if identifiable
3. **High-flow oxygen** 15L/min via NRB mask
4. **Large-bore IV access** × 2
5. **Cardiac monitoring**

#### Secondary Treatment
1. **Albuterol nebulizer** 2.5-5mg for bronchospasm
2. **H1 antihistamine:** Diphenhydramine 25-50mg IV
3. **H2 antihistamine:** Ranitidine 50mg IV
4. **Corticosteroids:** Methylprednisolone 125mg IV

#### Refractory Anaphylaxis
- **Repeat epinephrine** q5-15 minutes PRN
- **Epinephrine infusion** 2-10 mcg/min
- **Vasopressin** 0.01-0.04 units/min
- **Glucagon** 1-5mg IV (if on beta-blockers)

### 5.5 Diabetic Emergency Protocols

#### DKA Management
**Diagnostic Criteria:**
- Glucose >250 mg/dL
- pH <7.3 or bicarbonate <15 mEq/L
- Positive ketones

**Treatment Protocol:**
1. **Fluid resuscitation:** 0.9% NS 1-1.5L/hr × 1-2 hours
2. **Insulin therapy:** Regular insulin 0.1 units/kg/hr IV
3. **Potassium replacement:** Based on serum levels
4. **Bicarbonate:** Only if pH <7.0

**Monitoring:**
- Blood glucose q1 hour
- Basic metabolic panel q2-4 hours
- Arterial blood gas q4 hours
- Neurological status q2 hours

#### Hypoglycemia Protocol
**Conscious Patient:**
- Oral glucose 15-20g (glucose tablets/juice)
- Recheck glucose in 15 minutes
- Repeat if <70 mg/dL

**Unconscious/Unable to Take PO:**
- Dextrose 25g IV (50ml of D50W)
- Glucagon 1mg IM if no IV access
- Recheck glucose in 15 minutes

### 5.6 Respiratory Emergency Protocols

#### Severe Asthma Exacerbation
**Immediate Treatment:**
1. **Albuterol:** 2.5-5mg nebulizer q20 min × 3
2. **Ipratropium:** 0.5mg nebulizer with first albuterol
3. **Prednisolone:** 40-50mg PO or IV equivalent
4. **Oxygen** to maintain saturation ≥90%

**Severe/Life-threatening Criteria:**
- Peak flow <33% predicted
- Cannot complete sentences
- HR >120 bpm, RR >30/min
- Use of accessory muscles

**Additional Treatment:**
- **Magnesium sulfate:** 2g IV over 20 minutes
- **Continuous albuterol** 10-15mg/hr
- **Consider intubation** if deteriorating

#### COPD Exacerbation
**Assessment:**
- Arterial blood gas
- Chest X-ray
- Basic metabolic panel

**Treatment:**
1. **Controlled oxygen:** Target saturation 88-92%
2. **Albuterol/Ipratropium:** Combined nebulizer q6h
3. **Prednisolone:** 30-40mg daily × 5 days
4. **Antibiotics** if purulent sputum

### 5.7 Trauma Protocols

#### Primary Survey (ABCDE)
**A - Airway with C-spine protection**
- Assess patency
- Consider intubation if compromised
- Maintain cervical immobilization

**B - Breathing and ventilation**
- Inspect, palpate, auscultate
- Treat pneumothorax immediately
- Oxygen supplementation

**C - Circulation with hemorrhage control**
- Control obvious bleeding
- Assess pulse, blood pressure
- Large-bore IV access × 2

**D - Disability (neurological assessment)**
- GCS evaluation
- Pupillary response
- Motor function assessment

**E - Exposure and environment**
- Complete undressing
- Prevent hypothermia
- Log roll with spine protection

#### Hemorrhagic Shock Protocol
**Class I (15% blood loss):**
- Normal vital signs
- Crystalloid replacement

**Class II (15-30% blood loss):**
- Tachycardia, narrow pulse pressure
- Crystalloid 2-3L, consider blood

**Class III (30-40% blood loss):**
- Hypotension, tachycardia
- Immediate blood transfusion

**Class IV (>40% blood loss):**
- Shock, altered mental status
- Massive transfusion protocol

## 6. Medication Protocols

### High-Alert Medications

| Medication | Indication | Dose | Special Considerations |
|------------|------------|------|----------------------|
| Epinephrine | Anaphylaxis, arrest | 0.3-0.5mg IM | Auto-injector preferred |
| Insulin | DKA, hyperglycemia | 0.1 units/kg/hr | Continuous monitoring |
| Heparin | Anticoagulation | Weight-based nomogram | Monitor aPTT |
| Warfarin reversal | ICH, major bleeding | Vitamin K, PCC | INR monitoring |
| Amiodarone | Arrhythmias | 150mg IV over 10 min | Cardiac monitoring |

### Emergency Drug Calculations

**Weight-based Dosing:**
- Epinephrine: 0.01mg/kg (max 0.5mg)
- Atropine: 0.02mg/kg (min 0.1mg, max 0.5mg)
- Adenosine: 0.1mg/kg (max 6mg first, 12mg second)

**Continuous Infusions:**
- Dopamine: 5-20 mcg/kg/min
- Norepinephrine: 0.1-0.5 mcg/kg/min
- Epinephrine: 0.1-0.5 mcg/kg/min
