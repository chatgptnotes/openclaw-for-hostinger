## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To ensure 24/7 availability of essential emergency medications in appropriate quantities and locations throughout Hope & Ayushman Hospitals, enabling immediate treatment of life-threatening conditions.

**Scope:** This SOP covers:
- Emergency medication formulary
- Storage and distribution systems
- Inventory management
- Quality assurance procedures
- Access and security protocols

**Applicable to:**
- Emergency department staff
- Pharmacy personnel
- Nursing staff
- Critical care teams
- Night duty personnel

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Emergency Medication:** Drugs required for immediate life-saving treatment
- **Crash Cart:** Mobile unit containing emergency drugs and equipment
- **Code Blue Box:** Medication kit for cardiac arrest situations
- **High-Alert Medication:** Drugs with high risk of causing patient harm
- **PAR Level:** Predetermined minimum stock quantity

## 6. Storage Requirements

### 6.1 Temperature-Controlled Storage

#### Refrigerated Medications (2-8°C)
- **Insulin** (all types)
- **Vaccines** and immunoglobulins
- **Some antibiotics** (ampicillin injection)
- **Blood products** and albumin

**Monitoring Requirements:**
- Continuous temperature monitoring
- Alarm system for temperature deviations
- Backup refrigeration available
- Temperature logs maintained

#### Room Temperature Storage (15-25°C)
- Most emergency medications
- Controlled humidity (45-65%)
- Protection from light
- Secure storage areas

#### Special Storage Requirements

| Medication | Storage Requirement | Rationale |
|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Nitroglycerin** | Cool, dark place | Light/heat sensitive |
| **Epinephrine** | Protected from light | Photodegradation |
| **Insulin** | Refrigerated | Protein stability |
| **Blood products** | 1-6°C with monitoring | Safety requirements |

### 6.2 Security Measures

#### Controlled Substances (Schedule II-V)
- **Double-locked storage** (two different locks)
- **Access log maintained** with signatures
- **Narcotic count** performed each shift
- **Discrepancy reporting** protocol
- **Disposal documentation** for expired/waste

#### High-Alert Medications
- **Separate storage** from look-alike drugs
- **Color-coded labels** and alerts
- **Double verification** before administration
- **Tall man lettering** for similar names

## 7. Distribution Systems

### 7.1 Crash Cart Medication Systems

#### Cart Locations
- **Emergency Department:** 2 carts
- **ICU/CCU:** 1 cart each
- **General Wards:** 1 cart per floor
- **Operating Theatre:** 1 cart
- **Pediatric Ward:** 1 specialized cart

#### Cart Management
- **Sealed system** with tamper-evident seals
- **Exchange carts** when seal broken
- **24-hour restocking** commitment
- **Monthly expiry date checks**
- **Quality assurance audits**

#### Cart Contents Verification

| Check | Frequency | Responsible Person |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Seal integrity** | Daily | Charge Nurse |
| **Expiry dates** | Weekly | Pharmacist |
| **Drug levels** | After each use | Code team |
| **Equipment function** | Daily | Biomedical Engineer |

### 7.2 Emergency Drug Boxes

#### Code Blue Boxes
- **Pre-positioned** in all clinical areas
- **Standardized contents** across hospital
- **Clear labeling** with drug concentrations
- **Immediate replacement** after use

#### Pediatric Emergency Boxes
- **Weight-based dosing** charts included
- **Age-appropriate** concentrations
- **Specialized equipment** for children
- **Color-coded** for different weight ranges

### 7.3 Pharmacy Services

#### 24/7 Pharmacy Coverage
- **Day shift:** Full pharmacy services (7 AM - 7 PM)
- **Night coverage:** On-call pharmacist (7 PM - 7 AM)
- **Weekend coverage:** Duty pharmacist available
- **Holiday coverage:** Emergency contact system

#### Emergency Medication Access
- **After-hours cabinet** with essential drugs
- **Electronic access** with audit trail
- **Duty manager** authorization required
- **24-hour restocking** guarantee

### 8.1 Inventory Management

#### Daily Monitoring
- **Stock level checks** in all locations
- **Expiry date verification** (FEFO system)
- **Temperature log** review
- **Refrigeration alarm** checks

#### Weekly Reviews
- **Usage pattern** analysis
- **Reorder point** adjustments
- **Slow-moving item** identification
- **Waste minimization** strategies

#### Monthly Audits
- **Complete inventory** count
- **Expiry date** compliance review
- **Storage condition** verification
- **Documentation audit**

### 8.2 Medication Safety

#### Error Prevention
- **Look-alike/sound-alike** drug separation
- **High-alert medication** protocols
- **Tall man lettering** implementation
- **Barcode scanning** where available

#### Near-Miss Reporting
- **Voluntary reporting** system
- **No-blame culture** encouragement
- **Root cause analysis** for events
- **System improvements** implementation

### 8.3 Quality Indicators

| Indicator | Target | Monitoring Frequency |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| **Stock-out incidents** | Zero | Daily |
| **Expired medications found** | <1% | Weekly |
| **Temperature excursions** | <2 per month | Continuous |
| **Medication errors** | <1 per 1000 doses | Monthly |
| **Response time to requests** | <15 minutes | Real-time |

## 9. Emergency Procedures

### 9.1 After-Hours Medication Access

#### Urgent Medication Request Process
1. **Clinical assessment** by attending physician
2. **Duty manager notification** with clinical justification
3. **Pharmacy cabinet access** with dual verification
4. **Documentation** of medication removed
5. **Next-day pharmacist review** of appropriateness

#### Authorization Levels

| Medication Category | Authorization Required |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Non-controlled substances** | Duty nurse manager |
| **Controlled substances** | Duty medical officer |
| **High-cost medications** | Medical superintendent |
| **Investigational drugs** | Principal investigator |

### 9.2 Stock Emergency Procedures

#### Stock-Out Protocol
1. **Immediate notification** to pharmacy
2. **Alternative medication** identification
3. **Urgent procurement** from nearby facilities
4. **Therapeutic substitution** if approved
5. **Root cause analysis** completion

#### Supply Chain Disruption
- **Alternative supplier** activation
- **Therapeutic interchange** protocols
- **Conservative usage** guidelines
- **Priority allocation** to critical areas
