## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To establish a systematic triage process that ensures rapid assessment and prioritization of emergency patients based on severity of condition, optimizing resource utilization and patient outcomes.

**Scope:** This SOP covers:
- Triage assessment methodology
- Patient prioritization criteria
- Resource allocation procedures
- Documentation requirements
- Staff competency standards

**Applicable to:**
- Triage nurses
- Emergency department staff
- Casualty Medical Officers (CMOs)
- ED support staff

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Triage:** Systematic assessment process to determine treatment priority
- **ESI:** Emergency Severity Index (5-level triage system)
- **Acuity:** Degree of illness severity requiring immediate attention
- **Re-triage:** Reassessment of patient condition during ED stay
- **Fast-track:** Expedited process for minor injuries/illnesses

---

## 3. Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Triage Nurse | Primary triage assessment, patient prioritization |
| ED In-Charge | Triage system oversight, quality monitoring |
| CMO | Secondary assessment, treatment decisions |
| Registration Staff | Patient flow coordination |
| Security Officer | Crowd control, patient guidance |

---

## 4. Policy Statement

Hope & Ayushman Hospitals implements a standardized triage system to ensure all emergency patients are assessed within 5 minutes of arrival and prioritized appropriately based on clinical severity, not arrival time or payment capacity.

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Triage Area Setup

#### Physical Requirements
- Dedicated triage station at ED entrance
- Examination room with privacy
- Triage desk with computer terminal
- Vital signs monitoring equipment
- Color-coded triage tags/wristbands
- Emergency medication cart nearby

#### Equipment Checklist

| Equipment | Quantity | Check Frequency |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Blood pressure monitor | 2 | Every shift |
| Digital thermometer | 3 | Every shift |
| Pulse oximeter | 2 | Every shift |
| Weight scale | 1 | Daily |
| Glucose meter | 1 | Every shift |
| Peak flow meter | 1 | Daily |
| Stethoscope | 2 | Weekly |
| Penlight | 3 | Weekly |

### 5.2 Triage Assessment Process

#### Step 1: Initial Contact
- **Timeframe:** Within 1 minute of arrival
- **Duration:** <5 minutes for triage completion

1. Greet patient/family professionally
2. Obtain brief chief complaint
3. Visual assessment for immediate threats
4. Initiate triage documentation

#### Step 2: Primary Assessment (ABCDE)
**A - Airway:** Patent, obstructed, or threatened
**B - Breathing:** Rate, depth, effort, oxygen saturation
**C - Circulation:** Pulse, blood pressure, perfusion
**D - Disability:** Neurological status, GCS
**E - Exposure:** Temperature, visible injuries

#### Step 3: Vital Signs Documentation

| Vital Sign | Normal Ranges (Adults) | Action Required |
|------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| Heart Rate | 60-100 bpm | <50 or >120: Urgent |
| Blood Pressure | <140/90 mmHg | >180/110 or <90/60: Urgent |
| Respiratory Rate | 12-20/min | <10 or >24: Urgent |
| Temperature | 36.1-37.2°C | >38.5°C or <35°C: Urgent |
| Oxygen Saturation | >95% | <92%: Urgent |
| Pain Scale | 0-10 | >7: Consider priority |

### 5.3 ESI Triage Categories

#### Category 1 (RED) - Immediate
**Target Time to CMO:** Immediate
**Examples:**
- Cardiac arrest
- Respiratory failure
- Severe trauma with unstable vitals
- Stroke with acute deficits
- Anaphylaxis
- Active seizures

**Actions:**
1. Activate emergency response team
2. Direct to resuscitation bay
3. Initiate life-saving measures
4. Notify CMO immediately

#### Category 2 (ORANGE) - Emergent  
**Target Time to CMO:** <15 minutes
**Examples:**
- Chest pain with cardiac risk
- Severe respiratory distress
- Altered mental status
- Major trauma with stable vitals
- Severe pain (8-10/10)
- GI bleeding

**Actions:**
1. Prioritize for immediate room
2. Continuous monitoring
3. IV access preparation
4. Notify CMO within 5 minutes

#### Category 3 (YELLOW) - Urgent
**Target Time to CMO:** <30 minutes
**Examples:**
- Moderate pain (5-7/10)
- Minor trauma
- Fever >101°F
- Vomiting/diarrhea with dehydration
- Cellulitis
- Psychiatric complaints

**Actions:**
1. Regular monitoring q30 minutes
2. Assign to available room
3. Routine CMO notification

#### Category 4 (GREEN) - Less Urgent
**Target Time to CMO:** <60 minutes
**Examples:**
- Minor lacerations
- Rash without distress
- Cold symptoms
- Chronic pain flare
- Medication refills
- Minor sprains

**Actions:**
1. Fast-track if resources available
2. Re-triage q60 minutes
3. Patient education on wait times

#### Category 5 (BLUE) - Non-Urgent
**Target Time to CMO:** <120 minutes
**Examples:**
- Routine prescription needs
- Minor skin conditions
- Chronic stable conditions
- Health certificates
- Non-urgent referrals

**Actions:**
1. Consider redirection to OPD
2. Educate on appropriate care settings
3. Schedule if possible

### 5.4 Special Populations

#### Pediatric Patients (<16 years)
- Use pediatric vital sign norms
- Consider developmental factors
- Parental anxiety assessment
- Fast-track for fever >102°F
- Lower threshold for higher acuity

**Pediatric Vital Sign Ranges:**

| Age | HR (bpm) | RR (/min) | SBP (mmHg) |
|-----|----------|-----------|------------|
| 0-3 months | 100-180 | 30-60 | 65-85 |
| 3-24 months | 80-150 | 24-40 | 70-100 |
| 2-10 years | 70-120 | 18-30 | 80-110 |
| 10-16 years | 60-110 | 14-22 | 90-120 |

#### Geriatric Patients (>65 years)
- Medication review mandatory
- Fall risk assessment
- Cognitive screening
- Social support evaluation
- Higher index of suspicion for serious illness

#### Mental Health Patients
- Safety assessment priority
- De-escalation techniques
- Security availability
- Environmental awareness
- Trauma-informed approach

### 5.5 Re-triage Protocol

#### Triggers for Re-triage
1. Patient complaint of worsening
2. Vital sign deterioration
3. Extended wait time (>30 min beyond target)
4. Family/staff concerns
5. New symptom development

#### Re-triage Frequency
- Category 1: Continuous monitoring
- Category 2: Every 15 minutes
- Category 3: Every 30 minutes  
- Category 4: Every 60 minutes
- Category 5: Every 2 hours

### 5.6 Communication Procedures

#### Patient Communication
1. Explain triage process and wait times
2. Provide realistic expectations
3. Instructions for when to alert staff
4. Pain management strategies
5. Comfort measures available

#### Staff Communication
1. Clear handoffs to CMO
2. Documentation of all assessments
3. Alert for category upgrades
4. Family notification procedures

#### Documentation Requirements

| Document | Content | Timeline |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Triage Form | Assessment findings, vital signs, acuity | During triage |
| Re-triage Notes | Changes in condition, new acuity | Each re-assessment |
| Handoff Report | Complete assessment summary | At transfer to CMO |

## 9. Special Situations

### Mass Casualty Events
1. Activate disaster triage protocol
2. Implement START (Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment)
3. Establish triage officer role
4. Use colored triage tags
5. Document minimal essential information

### Infectious Disease Outbreaks
1. Screening questions at triage
2. Isolation precautions activation
3. PPE utilization
4. Separate waiting areas
5. Public health notification

### VIP/Media Attention Cases
1. Standard triage criteria apply
2. Privacy protection measures
3. Security coordination
4. Administrative notification
5. Media management protocol

## 10. Escalation Procedures

| Situation | Escalate To | Timeline |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Disagreement with triage level | ED In-Charge | Immediate |
| Extended wait times | Nurse Manager | 30 minutes |
| Violent/aggressive patient | Security + CMO | Immediate |
| Equipment malfunction | Biomedical Engineering | <15 minutes |
| Staffing shortage | Nursing Supervisor | Immediate |
