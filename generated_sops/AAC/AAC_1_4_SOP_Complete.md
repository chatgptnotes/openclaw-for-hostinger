## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Objective:** To establish standardized resuscitation procedures ensuring rapid, coordinated, and evidence-based response to cardiac and respiratory emergencies at Hope & Ayushman Hospitals.

**Scope:** This SOP covers:
- Adult and pediatric cardiopulmonary resuscitation
- Advanced cardiac life support protocols
- Team coordination and roles
- Equipment management
- Post-resuscitation care
- Documentation and quality improvement

**Applicable to:**
- All hospital clinical staff
- Code Blue team members
- Emergency department staff
- ICU personnel
- Security and support staff

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Cardiac Arrest:** Sudden cessation of cardiac pump function
- **Code Blue:** Emergency response for cardiac/respiratory arrest
- **ROSC:** Return of Spontaneous Circulation
- **VF:** Ventricular Fibrillation
- **VT:** Ventricular Tachycardia
- **PEA:** Pulseless Electrical Activity
- **Asystole:** Absence of cardiac electrical activity

## 5. Code Blue Activation

### 5.1 Recognition Criteria

#### Immediate Code Blue Activation
- **Cardiac arrest:** No pulse, no breathing
- **Respiratory arrest:** No breathing with pulse
- **Severe respiratory distress:** Unable to maintain airway
- **Unconsciousness with vital sign compromise**
- **Witnessed collapse**

#### Code Blue Activation Process
1. **Call Code Blue:** Dial 777 (internal) or press emergency button
2. **Start CPR immediately** if indicated
3. **Clear announcement:** "Code Blue, [Location], Code Blue, [Location]"
4. **Repeat announcement** every 2 minutes until response

### 5.2 Response Time Targets
- **Code Blue Team arrival:** <3 minutes
- **Defibrillator availability:** <2 minutes  
- **Advanced airway equipment:** <5 minutes
- **ICU bed availability:** <30 minutes

## 6. Basic Life Support (BLS)

### 6.1 Adult BLS Algorithm

#### Step 1: Check Responsiveness
- Tap shoulders firmly
- Shout "Are you okay?"
- Check for normal breathing (≤10 seconds)

#### Step 2: Call for Help
- Activate emergency response
- Request AED/defibrillator
- Assign roles to responders

#### Step 3: Check Pulse
- Carotid pulse check (≤10 seconds)
- If no pulse or unsure, begin CPR

#### Step 4: Begin CPR
**Chest Compressions:**
- Hand placement: Lower half of breastbone
- Compression depth: 5-6 cm (2-2.4 inches)
- Compression rate: 100-120/minute
- Allow complete recoil
- Minimize interruptions

**Ventilations:**
- 30 compressions : 2 ventilations
- Each breath: 1 second duration
- Visible chest rise
- Use bag-mask if available

#### Step 5: Continue Until
- ROSC achieved
- Advanced help arrives
- Provider becomes exhausted
- Medical direction to stop

### 6.2 Pediatric BLS Modifications

#### Age-Specific Techniques

| Age Group | Compressions | Depth | Rate | Ratio |
|-----------|--------------|-------|------|-------|
| Infant (0-1 year) | 2 thumbs or 2 fingers | 1/3 AP diameter | 100-120/min | 30:2 |
| Child (1-8 years) | Heel of 1 hand | 1/3 AP diameter | 100-120/min | 30:2 |
| Adolescent (>8 years) | 2 hands | 5-6 cm | 100-120/min | 30:2 |

#### Special Considerations
- **Airway:** Head tilt-chin lift (neutral position for infants)
- **Pulse check:** Brachial (infants), carotid/femoral (children)
- **Compressions:** Just below nipple line
- **Ventilations:** Just until chest rises

## 7. Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS)

### 7.1 Shockable Rhythms (VF/pVT)

#### Management Algorithm
1. **CPR immediately** - high-quality compressions
2. **Defibrillation ASAP** - biphasic 120-200J
3. **Resume CPR immediately** × 2 minutes
4. **Epinephrine 1mg IV** every 3-5 minutes
5. **Amiodarone 300mg IV** after 3rd shock
6. **Second dose amiodarone 150mg** if persistent

#### Defibrillation Protocol
- **Energy levels:** 120-200J (biphasic), 360J (monophasic)
- **Pad placement:** Anterior-posterior or anterior-lateral
- **Safety:** "Clear, I'm clear, you're clear, shocking"
- **Immediate CPR** after shock (do not check pulse)

### 7.2 Non-Shockable Rhythms (PEA/Asystole)

#### Management Algorithm
1. **High-quality CPR** immediately
2. **Epinephrine 1mg IV** every 3-5 minutes
3. **Consider reversible causes (H's & T's)**
4. **Continue CPR** with minimal interruptions

#### Reversible Causes (H's & T's)

| H's | T's |
|-----|-----|
| Hypovolemia | Toxins/Poisons |
| Hypoxia | Tamponade (cardiac) |
| Hydrogen ions (acidosis) | Tension pneumothorax |
| Hyperkalemia/Hypokalemia | Thrombosis (coronary) |
| Hypothermia | Thrombosis (pulmonary) |

### 7.3 Post-Resuscitation Care

#### Immediate Actions (First 20 minutes)
1. **12-lead ECG** - assess for STEMI
2. **Chest X-ray** - verify tube placement, assess injuries
3. **Arterial blood gas** - assess ventilation/oxygenation
4. **Laboratory studies** - electrolytes, glucose, lactate
5. **Temperature management** - target 32-36°C
6. **Blood pressure optimization** - MAP >65 mmHg

#### Targeted Temperature Management
**Indications:**
- Comatose after ROSC
- Initially shockable rhythm
- Non-shockable rhythm with witnessed arrest

**Protocol:**
- **Target temperature:** 32-36°C
- **Duration:** 24 hours minimum
- **Rewarming:** 0.25°C/hour
- **Monitoring:** Continuous core temperature

## 8. Pediatric Advanced Life Support (PALS)

### 8.1 Pediatric Arrest Algorithm

#### Initial Assessment
1. **Check responsiveness** and breathing
2. **Pulse check:** <10 seconds (brachial for infants)
3. **Begin CPR** if no pulse or HR <60 with poor perfusion

#### Drug Dosing (Pediatric)

| Medication | Dose | Route | Frequency |
|------------|------|-------|-----------|
| Epinephrine | 0.01 mg/kg (0.1 mL/kg of 1:10,000) | IV/IO | Every 3-5 minutes |
| Amiodarone | 5 mg/kg | IV/IO | May repeat once |
| Lidocaine | 1-1.5 mg/kg | IV/IO | May repeat 0.5-0.75 mg/kg |
| Atropine | 0.02 mg/kg (min 0.1mg, max 0.5mg) | IV/IO | May repeat once |

#### Defibrillation (Pediatric)
- **Initial dose:** 2-4 J/kg
- **Subsequent doses:** 4 J/kg
- **Maximum:** 10 J/kg or adult dose

### 8.2 Special Pediatric Considerations

#### Common Arrest Rhythms
- **Primary respiratory arrest** (most common)
- **Bradycardia with poor perfusion**
- **VF/pVT** (less common, usually secondary)

#### Age-Specific Equipment

| Age | Weight | ETT Size | Blade | Compressions |
|-----|--------|----------|-------|--------------|
| Newborn | 3 kg | 3.0-3.5 | Miller 0 | 2 thumbs |
| Infant (1-12 mo) | 3-10 kg | 3.5-4.0 | Miller 1 | 2 fingers/thumbs |
| Toddler (1-3 yr) | 10-14 kg | 4.0-4.5 | Miller 1-2 | Heel of hand |
| Preschool (3-5 yr) | 14-18 kg | 4.5-5.0 | Miller 2 | Heel of hand |
| School age (5-12 yr) | 18-36 kg | 5.0-6.0 | Miller 2-3 | Two hands |

## 9. Team Dynamics and Communication

### 9.1 Code Blue Team Roles

#### Team Leader
- **Assumes command** upon arrival
- **Directs resuscitation efforts**
- **Makes clinical decisions**
- **Communicates with family**
- **Calls time of death if applicable**

#### Primary CPR Provider
- **Performs chest compressions**
- **Ensures proper depth and rate**
- **Switches every 2 minutes**
- **Maintains compression fraction >80%**

#### Airway Manager
- **Manages ventilation**
- **Places advanced airway**
- **Confirms tube placement**
- **Monitors end-tidal CO2**

#### Defibrillation Operator
- **Analyzes rhythm**
- **Charges defibrillator**
- **Ensures safety**
- **Delivers shocks**

#### Medication Nurse
- **Prepares medications**
- **Administers drugs per orders**
- **Monitors IV access**
- **Reports drug administrations**

#### Recorder/Timekeeper
- **Documents all events**
- **Tracks timing of interventions**
- **Monitors duration of efforts**
- **Calls out next drug times**

### 9.2 Communication Protocols

#### Closed-Loop Communication
1. **Team leader gives order** clearly
2. **Receiver acknowledges** and repeats back
3. **Action is performed**
4. **Completion is reported** to leader

#### Examples
- "Give 1mg epinephrine IV"
- "1mg epinephrine IV, copy"
- "1mg epinephrine given IV"

### 9.3 Debriefing Process

#### Hot Wash (Immediately post-event)
- **What went well?**
- **What could be improved?**
- **Any safety concerns?**
- **Equipment issues?**

#### Formal Debrief (Within 24-48 hours)
- Detailed case review
- Timeline analysis
- Quality metrics evaluation
- Learning points identification
- System improvements needed

## 13. Special Situations

### 13.1 Pregnancy-Related Cardiac Arrest

#### Modifications
- **Left uterine displacement** manually
- **Cesarean delivery** if >20 weeks and no ROSC in 4 minutes
- **Standard ACLS drugs** are safe
- **Higher compression rate** may be needed

### 13.2 Hypothermic Cardiac Arrest

#### Protocol Modifications
- **Continue resuscitation** until rewarmed
- **Limit shocks** to 3 until temp >30°C
- **Space medications** at longer intervals
- **Active rewarming** essential

### 13.3 Drug Overdose

#### Specific Antidotes
- **Naloxone:** Opioid overdose (0.4-2mg IV)
- **Flumazenil:** Benzodiazepine overdose (0.2mg IV)
- **Calcium:** Calcium channel blocker overdose
- **Glucagon:** Beta-blocker overdose

## 14. Family Communication

### 14.1 During Resuscitation
- **Assign family liaison** nurse
- **Provide regular updates** every 15 minutes
- **Explain procedures** being performed
- **Offer chaplain/social worker** support
- **Consider family presence** if appropriate

### 14.2 End-of-Life Discussions
- **Private setting** for communication
- **Clear, honest information** about prognosis
- **Time for questions** and decisions
- **Respect cultural/religious** preferences
- **Organ donation** discussion if appropriate
